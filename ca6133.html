All about A Day on the Green 

Starting in 2001 on Australia day in Victoria, A Day on the Green has grown to become one of the biggest musical traditions in Australia’s history! Hosted in wineries and venues in West Australia all the way to Australian Neighbour New Zealand, visiting one of these concerts should be on the destination wish-list of any Oceania music fan and even those who are visiting! A Day on the Green has played host to such great acts as Elton John, Neil Young with Crazy Horse, The Beach Boys, Meat Loaf, Joe Cocker, Alicia Keys, Tom Jones and many others! And the venues; let us not forget that these sets of concerts are hosted in some Australia’s most scenic locations, in seventeen wineries across the country.

Seeing as most international concerts take place in five of Australia’s great cities, most Australians are usually forced to travel to Perth, Brisbane, Adelaide, Sydney or Melbourne. A Day on the Green aims to change that with concerts occurring in Geelong, Newcastle and Mt. Cotton to name a few. Proof of the festival's success are concerts making it across The Tasman Sea, popping up in Auckland and Martinborough!
