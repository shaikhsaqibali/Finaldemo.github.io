Australia National Cricket Team history

Dating back to 1877, the Australian Cricket team are the joint oldest test cricket team, along with England, when the two countries played the first ever Test match together. Due to the long distance between the two countries, and Test cricket solely occurring between them, Test matches happened seldom and sparingly. Although Australia’s population paled in comparison with England, the team itself were very competitive in early games! Surprisingly, most cricketers at the time hailed from either New South Wales or Victoria! They are a force to be reckoned with in the world of cricket, regarded as the most successful Test cricket team of recent years, that's why Australia cricket team tickets tend to sell rapidly. 

There have been some truly great Australian cricketers over the years, none more so than Don Bradman. Sometimes referred to simply as 'The Don', Bradman is widely acknowledged as the greatest cricket batsman of all time. Don Bradman managed an incredible average of 99.94 over his test career, often called the greatest achievement by any sportsman in any major sport. Other Australian legends that have donned the 'Baggy Green' cap of the Australian national team include Ricky Ponting who is the leading run scorer for Australia in test cricket, Shane Warne who holds the record for most test wickets, fast bowlers Dennis Lillee and Glenn McGrath and wicket-keeper Adam Gilchrist. When you buy Australia National Cricket Team tickets you can see the current Australian crop play, some of whom will surely take their place on this list. 

Australia National Cricket Team roster

After a brief slide in the cricket rankings, Australia can now boast an exceptionally strong squad. The side's recent 2018 Ashes victory in Australia proved beyond doubt that the Australia National Cricket Team under the captaincy of Steve Smith is ready to take on where Ricky Ponting's great side left off. With the likes of David Warner aggressively opening the batting and fast bowlers Mitchell Starc and Josh Hazlewood terrorizing batsmen, this is one of the most exciting cricketing sides to watch.
